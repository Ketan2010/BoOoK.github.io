1.The Sellout

Auther:The Sellout,Humor

What this book is about:This satirical novel about race and racism reads like a brilliant standup routine that goes on for days. 3.Cold Comfort Farm

Auther:Stella Gibbons,Humor

What this book is about:Who knew living on a farm in England during the 1930s could be so rousingly funny? In this breezy satire of depressing rural novels (like Tess of the d’Urbevilles), metropolitan socialite Flora Poste moves in with her country relatives and turns their insular life on its head. It's a ridiculously funny read that has been making its readers laugh out loud for nearly 100 years.

About Auther:was an English author, journalist, and poet. She established her reputation with her first novel, Cold Comfort Farm (1932) which has been reprinted many times. Although she was active as a writer for half a century, none of her later 22 novels or other literary works—which included a sequel to Cold Comfort Farm—achieved the same critical or popular success.

7.Where’d You Go Bernadette

Auther:Maria Semple,Humor

What this book is about: Semple, a former TV writer for Arrested Development and Ellen, turns the family drama on its head in this hilarious twist-and-turn story. Two eccentric, tight-knit parents plan a trip to Antarctica to celebrate their middle school daughter’s straight-A streak. However, the plan wildly devolves from there when matriarch Bernadette goes missing. This modern epistolary novel will get the big screen treatment in fall 2018, with Cate Blanchette starring as the titular Bernadette.

About Auther: is an American novelist and screenwriter. She is the author of This One Is Mine (2008), Where'd You Go, Bernadette (2012), and Today Will Be Different (2016).Her television credits include Beverly Hills Mad About You, Saturday Night Live, Arrested Development, Suddenly Susan, and Ellen.
